How to Plant Flowers

Step 1: Prepare the Soil

Choose a well-draining area in your garden. Loosen the soil with a garden fork or shovel, removing any weeds or debris.

Step 2: Choose Your Flowers

Select flowers that thrive in your climate and soil type. Consider factors such as sunlight requirements and bloom time.

Step 3: Dig Planting Holes

Use a trowel or shovel to dig holes for your flowers. Make sure the holes are deep and wide enough to accommodate the plant's roots.

Step 4: Plant Your Flowers

Gently remove the flowers from their containers and place them in the prepared holes. Backfill with soil and pat down gently to remove air pockets.

Step 5: Water and Mulch

Water the newly planted flowers thoroughly to help them settle into their new environment. Apply a layer of mulch around the base of the plants to retain moisture and suppress weeds.
